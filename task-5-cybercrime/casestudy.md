# Case Study: The "Urgent Hospital Bill" UPI Fraud

**What the crime is:** UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud combined with social engineering and identity impersonation.

**How it typically happens:** The attacker begins by compromising a student’s social media account (such as Instagram or WhatsApp) or by creating a highly convincing clone account using their public photos. Once established, the attacker sends direct messages to the victim’s contacts—often college batchmates. The message creates a false sense of extreme urgency, claiming the 'friend' has been in a minor accident or is stuck at a hospital pharmacy without their physical wallet. The attacker pleads for an immediate UPI transfer of a specific amount (e.g., ₹2,500) to a provided number, promising to return it by evening. Because the message comes from a trusted profile and plays on the victim's empathy and panic, the target often bypasses normal logical checks and sends the money immediately without verifying via a phone call.

**Who is usually targeted:** College students are prime targets because they are highly active on digital payment platforms, have extensive online social networks, and frequently transfer small to medium sums to friends for shared expenses like food or cab rides.

**The consequences:** The primary consequence is immediate financial loss, as UPI transfers are instantaneous and incredibly difficult to reverse once authorized by the user with their PIN. Secondary consequences include severe reputational damage to the person whose identity was impersonated, and a lingering sense of anxiety and distrust among peer groups regarding digital communications.
