# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

1. **Verify Urgent Financial Requests (UPI Safety):** If a friend messages you asking for urgent money, always call them on their actual phone number to verify their voice and the situation before sending a single rupee.
2. **Never Share Your UPI PIN (UPI Safety):** Remember that a UPI PIN is only required to *send* money, never to *receive* money. If someone asks for your PIN to "authorize" funds they are sending to you, it is a scam.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts (College Email, GitHub, LinkedIn, Social Media) using an authenticator app, not just SMS.
4. **Check the Sender's Email Address:** When receiving official-looking emails (from banks or VIT administration), click on the sender's name to verify the actual email address matches the official domain.
5. **Use Unique Passwords:** Never use the same password for your VITyarthi portal and your social media accounts. Use a secure password manager.
6. **Keep Software Updated:** Regularly update your OS, browser, and antivirus software to patch known security vulnerabilities.
7. **Be Wary of Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or making UPI payments while connected to unsecured public college or cafe Wi-Fi networks.
8. **Report Incidents Immediately:** If you are a victim of financial fraud or cyberbullying, immediately report it to the **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)** and call the **Helpline 1930**.
