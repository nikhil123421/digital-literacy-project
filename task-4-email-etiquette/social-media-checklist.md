# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

### The Do's:
1. **Do audit your privacy settings regularly:** Ensure personal posts are restricted to friends/family, while professional achievements (like GitHub repos) are public.
2. **Do curate a professional network:** Actively connect with professors, alumni, and industry professionals on LinkedIn to build relationships early.
3. **Do share academic and personal projects:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to document your learning journey and showcase your tech stack and hackathon wins.
4. **Do verify information before sharing:** Fact-check news and articles to avoid spreading misinformation or damaging your online credibility.
5. **Do engage positively:** Leave constructive, encouraging comments on your peers' professional updates and project launches.

### The Don'ts:
1. **Don't post in the heat of the moment:** Avoid emotional rants or engaging in aggressive online arguments that leave a permanent negative footprint.
2. **Don't share sensitive university data:** Never post screenshots of proprietary code, internal university emails, or confidential exam/assignment details.
3. **Don't neglect your "About" sections:** Don't leave bios blank; always include a brief, professional summary of your current academic status and goals.
4. **Don't use inappropriate profile pictures:** Keep your photos on professional platforms (like LinkedIn, GitHub, and college portals) clean and approachable.
5. **Don't ignore the permanence of the internet:** Remember that even deleted posts can be screenshotted and recovered by employers or university administration.
